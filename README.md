# G.A.P.A.S
(Global Agricultural Payment and Asset Settlement)

## 🧩 Problem
Traditional agricultural financing systems in the Philippines remain inaccessible, slow, and heavily dependent on intermediaries, leaving many farmers without sufficient capital to fully utilize their farmland and sustain agricultural operations. Farmers often struggle to secure funding for seeds, fertilizers, labor, irrigation, feeds, and farming equipment, especially during planting and harvesting seasons.
Access to agricultural credit remains a major challenge among smallholder farmers in the Philippines. Studies show that many small-scale farmers continue to rely on informal lenders because access to formal credit institutions is limited due to collateral requirements, lack of financial records, and insufficient rural banking services (Llanto, 2005).
The agricultural financing gap in the Philippines is also significant. According to studies cited by the Agricultural Credit Policy Council (ACPC), the country has an estimated agricultural credit gap of approximately ₱366.6 billion, demonstrating the large unmet demand for accessible agricultural financing systems (Digal & Placencia, 2019).
Financial exclusion among Filipino farmers remains critically high. Based on reports from the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP), approximately 73% of workers in the agriculture sector do not own bank accounts, making farmers one of the most financially excluded sectors in the country (BSP, 2021). The primary reasons include insufficient income, lack of valid identification documents, and difficulty meeting banking requirements.
This issue is particularly alarming because agriculture remains a critical sector in the Philippine economy and food security system. In many cases, underutilized farmland exists not because land is unavailable, but because farmers lack sufficient seasonal operational capital to maximize agricultural productivity.
At the same time, investors and institutions face challenges in agricultural financing because traditional systems provide limited transparency, weak monitoring mechanisms, and inefficient settlement processes for agricultural investments. Agricultural operations are also highly exposed to risks caused by climate change, unstable weather conditions, pests, diseases, and fluctuating market prices (Dasco et al., 2019).
These limitations create a fragmented agricultural ecosystem where farmers struggle to access fair funding opportunities while investors face operational uncertainty and lack of transparency.

## 🌟 Vision
To build a transparent, accessible, and decentralized agricultural financing ecosystem in the Philippines where farmers can easily access fair capital without intermediaries, and investors can confidently fund agricultural production with full transparency, reduced risk, and automated settlement using blockchain technology.
In the long term, the project aims to reduce the agricultural credit gap, improve farm productivity, and promote financial inclusion for rural communities through Web3-powered finance.


## 🎯 Purpose
GAPAS (Global Agricultural Payment and Asset Settlement) is a blockchain-based agricultural finance platform built on Stellar that enables transparent agricultural funding, tokenized farm projects, and automated financial settlement. The platform allows verified farmers and agricultural cooperatives to register farming projects that can receive funding from investors using blockchain-based payments such as USDC on Stellar. Smart contracts powered by Soroban automate funding agreements, settlement distribution, and transaction transparency.
This system is strongly supported by related literature (RRL) showing that blockchain in agriculture improves transparency, efficiency, and farmer income. Nguyen Thi Thoi and M. Kavitha (2025) reported about 20% increase in farmer income due to reduced intermediaries. Aen Fariah (2025) found improvements in traceability (95%), efficiency (15%), and cost reduction (25%). A review by Acarya et al. (2024) highlighted that blockchain improves trust, traceability, and smart contract automation, while Kamilaris et al. (2023) emphasized better transparency and reduced information asymmetry in agro-food systems. Overall, studies show blockchain can increase income, improve traceability, and enhance efficiency and trust in agricultural finance systems.
This studies supports the features of GAPAS as this platform focuses on creating a secure and inclusive agricultural financing ecosystem by:
 • Connecting farmers and investors directly
 • Enabling tokenized agricultural funding
 • Providing transparent on-chain settlement
 • Supporting cooperative-based verification
 • Automating payout distribution using smart contracts
 • Allowing farmers to cash out blockchain earnings into real-world currency
The MVP focuses on verified farm registration, investor funding, cooperative validation, blockchain settlement, and transparent payout tracking.


## 👥 Target Users
Farmers (Primary Users)
 Farmers who need capital for seeds, fertilizers, labor, irrigation, and equipment but lack access to formal loans.
Agricultural Investors / Funding Partners
 Individuals or institutions who want to invest in agriculture with transparent tracking and lower risk through smart contracts.
Agricultural Cooperatives / NGOs (Secondary Users)
 Organizations that support farmers and can use the platform to distribute funds efficiently and monitor usage.


## ✨ Features
Smart Contract Escrow System — Funds are locked and released automatically based on farming milestones or conditions.
Transparent Investment Tracking — Investors can view where their funds are allocated and monitor farm progress.
Farmer Funding Requests — Farmers can submit funding needs for specific crops and seasons.
Risk Monitoring System — Basic tracking of crop cycles, delays, and environmental risks.
Decentralized Settlement (Stellar Soroban) — Secure and automated payment processing without intermediaries.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
Frontend: React / Next.js (for dashboard and user interface)
Backend: Node.js / Express (API handling and data processing)
Blockchain: Stellar Soroban Smart Contracts + Horizon API + Stellar SDK
Authentication: Wallet-based login (Freighter / Stellar wallet integration)
Other Tools: Docker, GitHub Actions (CI/CD), Figma (UI design)
